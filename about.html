What is CELC?

The Canadian Engineering Leadership Conference (CELC) is Canada's largest undergraduate engineering conference, bringing together student leaders from over 46 schools annually. Organized by the Canadian Federation of Engineering Students (CFES), CELC creates a dynamic space where passionate engineering students exchange ideas, develop leadership skills, and shape the future of the profession. Through interactive workshops, thought-provoking panels, engaging discussions, and social events, delegates learn from peers and industry leaders while exploring what success means to them. CELC 2026 challenges participants to rethink engineering leadership and inspires them to build a purposeful, inclusive, and sustainable future for the field.

Conference Theme

Success is not defined by one thing, rather, it's a dynamic composite of intersecting factors that will look different for each person. As engineers, it is important to define what we hope to achieve while considering the people we impact along the way to ensure our values align with our visions. This year's theme, Track to Success, invites delegates to discover their own version of success and traverse the winding pathways that can lead them towards their goals. Through collaborative workshops, honest conversations, and insightful lectures, we will lay the trackwork for shaping a successful future that is inclusive, sustainable, and purpose driven.

Governance

This track is intended for the VP externals (or equivalent position) from each engineering society. Sessions will include general assemblies, conference bidding, and elections facilitated by the CFES.

Professional Engineer

This track aims to equip delegates with essential tools for success as young professionals, helping them navigate shifting trends and address emergent challenges. Sessions will explore the intersection between technical work and social implications, highlighting a holistic human centric approach to engineering.

Leadership

This track will focus on leading with intentionality by exploring appropriate and sustainable leadership strategies that each delegate can implement within their communities. Discussions will center on collaborative approaches to leadership, fostering inclusivity within teams, and the value of diverse perspectives, ultimately encouraging delegates to reflect not only on how to lead, but also on why they lead and who they impact.
